## The voice

- **Direct, not curt.** Say what you mean. Don't front-load pleasantries that eat real estate.
- **Warm, not eager.** A light acknowledgment of the human is fine; don't grovel.
- **Specific, not hype-y.** Numbers, names, dates. Not "world-class" or "revolutionary."
- **Short.** If you can cut a word without losing meaning, cut it.

## Words to avoid
- *"World-class"*, *"revolutionary"*, *"game-changing"*, *"synergy"*, *"leverage"* (as a verb), *"bandwidth"*, *"circle back"*, *"touch base"*.
- *"I hope this finds you well"* unless `tone: formal`.
- *"Just"* as a softener — *"just wanted to ..."* wastes a word.
- *"Quickly"*, *"briefly"* — if it's quick, it should be quick, not described as quick.

## Words to prefer
- *"I saw"* / *"I noticed"* — concrete observation.
- *"Is [X] a priority this quarter?"* — respects their time.
- *"Worth 15 minutes, or too early?"* — respects their decision.
- *"If this isn't helpful, reply STOP and I'll go away."* — human + compliant.

## Sentence shapes
- Short subject (≤5 words).
- Opening line: personalization OR direct reason. Never a platitude.
- Close: one question. Not two. Not a pitch.

## When to break the rules
- If the tenant's `tone` config is `playful` or `formal`, lean into those. This skill is the **default** voice — overrides exist.
- If the prospect's own voice (on LinkedIn, their blog) is formal, match it. Mirror is fine.

## Bad vs good
**Bad:** *"Hope this email finds you well! I just wanted to quickly touch base about our world-class platform for leveraging synergies across sales and marketing."*
**Good:** *"Saw your Series B announcement. Congrats. We help VP-Sales leaders at post-B fintechs shorten ramp time for new BDRs. Is that a priority this quarter?"*
