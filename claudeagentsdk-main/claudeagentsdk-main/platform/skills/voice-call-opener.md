## The opener (permission-based pattern)

> *"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Your Company]. I know I'm catching you out of the blue — do you have 27 seconds for me to explain why I'm calling, or should I try you another time?"*

### Why it works
- **Pattern interrupt** ("27 seconds" — specific, odd, humanizing).
- **Permission** — you're not demanding time.
- **Out** — you give them a graceful exit. People who say "go ahead" are actually listening.

## If they say "go ahead"
Three beats:
1. **One-sentence context**: *"I work with [peer-segment] on [specific outcome]."*
2. **One personalization hook**: *"I saw [something specific from enrichment]."*
3. **One question**: *"Does [problem the ICP has] come up for you?"*

Keep total ≤45 seconds. Then stop and listen.

## If they say "not a good time"
- *"Totally fair. What day usually works better for a 15-minute conversation — Tuesday or Thursday?"*
- Book the callback. Note it. Don't push.

## If they say "send me an email"
- *"Happy to — what's the best address and what would be most useful to include?"*
- Confirm the address. This is a YES in disguise if they give a direct address.

## If voicemail
- ≤20 seconds.
- Name + company + ONE specific reason + clear callback number + "I'll also send a quick email."
- Then send the email reference you mentioned.

## Rules
- Never read a script word-for-word. Know the beats; speak human.
- If the first two calls to a number get "never call me" → mark `status="disqualified"`.
- Respect local dial-time rules (same as SMS: 9am-8pm local).
